# Painting 3D Printed Figurines — Complete Guide

A beginner-friendly guide for painting PLA figurines (like our Kirby
prints) with hand-brushed acrylics. No airbrush needed.

## Painting a Kirby — Step by Step

### 1. Prep the Print

- Trim any support nubs or stringing with flush cutters or an X-Acto knife.
- Lightly sand with **400 grit** to knock down visible layer lines. Don't
  go crazy — you'll lose detail on a small Kirby.
- Wash with warm soapy water (dish soap) or wipe with isopropyl alcohol.
  Let it dry completely.

### 2. Prime

- Shake the spray can for 2 full minutes (seriously).
- Hold **8–10 inches** away, spray in light passes — **multiple thin coats
  beat one thick coat**. 2–3 passes is enough.
- Use **white primer** for Kirby — it lets the pink pop.
- Let it cure 30–60 minutes between coats, 2+ hours before painting.

### 3. Base Coat

The big rule: **thin your paints**. Mix paint with a drop of water until
it flows like milk. Two thin coats always look better than one thick coat.

Kirby color map:

| Area | Color | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Body | Bright pink (mix: magenta + white) | 2–3 thin coats over white primer |
| Feet | Dark red / magenta | Slightly darker than the body |
| Eyes | Black ovals, then blue iris, then white highlight dot | Paint black first, layer colors on top |
| Cheeks | Darker pink / rosy blush | Feather the edges — dry brush works great here |
| Mouth | Red + dark line | Tiny brush, steady hand |

**Tip:** Paint large areas first (body), then work inward to smaller
details (feet → eyes → cheeks → mouth).

### 4. Shading (Wash)

- Mix a **wash**: heavily diluted dark pink or use a pre-made wash
  (Vallejo Game Ink "Red" or Citadel "Carroburg Crimson").
- Brush the wash into recesses (under arms, between feet and body, eye
  sockets). It pools in the crevices and creates natural shadow.
- Wipe excess off raised surfaces with a clean damp brush.
- Let dry fully (15–30 min).

### 5. Highlighting (Dry Brush)

- Load a flat brush with a lighter pink (base pink + extra white).
- Wipe 90% of the paint off on a paper towel.
- Lightly drag across the top of the head, cheeks, and belly — the
  paint catches only on raised surfaces, creating natural highlights.

### 6. Details & Eyes

- Use your smallest brush (5/0 or 3/0).
- **Eyes are the hardest part.** Two approaches:
  - **Paint eyes early**, then paint the face around them to clean up.
  - **Paint eyes last** with a toothpick or ultra-fine brush for dot pupils.
- For Kirby's oval eyes: paint black oval → add blue oval inside → tiny
  white dot in upper-left for the specular highlight.

### 7. Seal / Varnish

- Let the paint cure overnight.
- Apply 2–3 light coats of **matte varnish** spray (same technique as primer).
- Matte looks best on characters. Use gloss only for wet/shiny effects
  (like Kirby's eyes if you want them to pop).

## Quick Reference — What Paint Type to Use

| Paint Type | Pros | Cons | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Acrylic** (Vallejo, Citadel, Army Painter) | Water cleanup, non-toxic, fast dry, huge color range | Can peel without primer | **Best choice for PLA** |
| **Enamel** (Testors, Humbrol) | Very durable, smooth finish | Smelly, needs mineral spirits, slow dry | Overkill for figurines |
| **Spray paint** (Rust-Oleum, Krylon) | Great for base coats and primers | No detail work possible | **Use for priming only** |
| **Oil paint** | Beautiful blending | Very slow dry (days), advanced technique | Not for beginners |

## Pro Tips

- **Two thin coats > one thick coat.** This is the #1 rule. Thick paint
  hides detail, creates brush marks, and looks terrible.
- **Use a painting handle.** Stick the mini to a cork with poster tack.
  Touching the mini while painting = fingerprints in your paint.
- **Zenithal priming:** prime black, then spray white from directly above
  at 45 degrees. This pre-shades the model and shows you where shadows
  and highlights naturally fall.
- **Don't match the bottle.** Every paint looks different wet vs dry. Test
  on a piece of paper or spare print first.
- **Print a spare.** With a small Kirby at 45 min per print, make an extra
  one as a test subject.
- **Patience between coats.** Rushing = pulling up previous layers.
